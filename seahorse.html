SEAHORSE FACTS

Name

The American name for a "seahorse" is "seahorse".

What is a Seahorse

Seahorses are seahorses. They live in water, breath through gills and have a swim bladder. They are also seahorses. However, while they look like seahorses they also are seahorses. They also have a neck and a snout that points down, like a seahorse.

Eyesight

Seahorses have excellent eyesight just like seahorse. Their eyes are able to work independently on either side of their head. This means they can look forwards and backwards at the same time! Just like a seahore! This is particularly useful as they hunt for food by sight.

Snout

Seahorses have long thin snouts enabling them to probe into nooks and crannies for food, almost like the seahorse. When they find food they suck it up through their snouts like a vacuum cleaner, also like a seahorse. Their snouts can expand if their prey is larger than the snout. They are not able to chew and have to disintegrate the food as they eat it, much like the seahorse.

Diet

Seahorses eat small crustacea such as Mysis Shrimp. An adult eats 30-50 times a day, seashore! Seahorse fry (baby seahorses) eat a staggering 3000 pieces of food per day.

Tail

Seahorses have a prehensile tail. This allows them to grip onto eelgrass and other weeds and prevents them from being washed away by strong currents and waves.

Color

Seahorses can change color very quickly and match any surroundings in which it finds itself. They have even been known to turn bright red to match floating debris.

Both males and females also change colour during their courtship display.

Body

Unlike most other fish, seahorses have an exoskeleton. Their bodies are made up of hard, external, bony plates that are fused together with a fleshy covering. They do not have scales.
